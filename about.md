I am Jayanth, Assistant Professor of Healthcare Logistics and Vice-Director of the Digitalization Platform at KTH Royal Institute of Technology. I build models to improve decision making in complex processes. The models are either simulation-based, or data driven or participatory, but more often than not are a combination of the three. I work mainly in healthcare, but have previously worked in urban planning, transportation, energy, agriculture and disaster management. 

I teach courses on Applied Machine Learning, at the first, second and third cycle levels (Bachelors, Masters and PhD). I also teach modules on Simulation Thinking, Agent-Based Modeling, AI/ML ethics, Health Informatics and Logictics for various other courses. 

I also help develop data sharing infrastructure at KTH, to align with stewardship and curation practices. I also help develop a network for ECRs (Early Career Researchers) in Digitalization at KTH, to provide mentorship, funding, project and other oppurtunities. 

In a previous life, I worked at CSTEP, India and am an alum of IIIT-B. 

As an academic with a not-so-traditional academic background, I often **help/guide** researchers on getting into PhD programs, on writing cover letters and tailoring CVs for PhD or Post-Doc positions and getting started on a self-directed research career. If you or someone you know want to pick my brain, send me an email. 

#### About this site:
This space is mostly to catalog my work and projects, and to serve as a blog and test some ideas. You can find my CV and a summary of my work on LinkedIN.
